### Subnetting Trick

Since we are looking at the last octet, let's make it bigger:

![450](../_assets/Pasted%20image%2020260518180939.png)
![ 450](../_assets/Pasted%20image%2020260518181039.png)

Notice that the last bit portion is **64**. This means that to find the next subnet, we just have to add 64!

* The net address of subnet #1 is `192.168.1.0/26`.
* To get the net address of subnet #2, add 64 to subnet #1, which will give us `192.168.1.64/26`
* To get the net address of subnet #3, add 64 to subnet #2 , which will give us `192.168.1.128/26`
* To get the net address of subnet #4, add 64 to subnet #3 , which will give us `192.168.1.192/26`

By adding 64 each time, we were able to find the network addresses of each subnet.
